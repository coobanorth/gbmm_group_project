What Is Information Security?

Information security is the practice of protecting information by mitigating information risks. It is an important part of information risk management.

CIA or Confidentiality Integrity and Availability is the focus of information security and how we can keep all three protected without slowing down productivity

Risk management with CIA

  • Identifying information and assets as well as potential threats
  • Evaluating the risks
  • Addressing the risks
  • Monitoring the system and making changes to ensure security

Threats to information security

  • the most common threats today are software attacks, malicious code like viruses, phishing scams, trojan horses and ect.
  • It doesnt just concern software threats natural disasters like earth quakes and floods can destroy storage devices which could risk the integrity of the data.
  • An other threat related to information security is identity theft and hackers will use social engineering to gain access to systems by impersonating a trusted figure for example a manager or IT technician

Responces to threats

  • Reduce - reduce the amount of vulnerabilities in the system
  • Outource - put the cost onto another entity like insurance
  • accept - some vulnerabilities will lose less money then you would save by fixing

How this relates to you?

Big companies working with your data in the uk have to follow data protection laws. So if your data is stolen, lost or sold ilegally they could be liable to fines

Almost all big websites store information from users so there is a high chance your data could be stolen or sold. This data can be quite harmless stuff like frequently visited site and viewing history to quite sensitive data like bank details and addresses.

knowing if your data has been leaked onto the internet is very important in mitigating risks online. sites like https://haveibeenpwned.com/ can check wether your email and password have been in a data breach

steps to take if your email has been compromised

  • Change your password - Use a password checker to see if its secure
  • sign out of all websites with your email
  • notify all contacts
  • Check if other accounts have been compromised
